## The Almond Metaphor

An almond tree grows from a single seed, splitting rock to reach sunlight. It blooms before other trees dare — sometimes too early, sometimes getting frosted. But it persists.

The word "almond" shares its root with "amygdala" — the brain's center for emotion and memory. This is not coincidence. Almondo is an emotional brain: remembering not just *what* happened in the code, but *how it felt* when the build broke at 2am, or when the feature finally worked.

Sweet almonds nourish. Bitter almonds contain amygdalin — a compound that's toxic in large doses but medicinal in small ones. Honest feedback works the same way. Too much criticism destroys. The right amount heals.
